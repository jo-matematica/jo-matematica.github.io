BI Cyclistic Bike-share Project

This was a business intelligence project from the Google Business Intelligence Certificate to analyze and visualize Cyclistic (fictional) bike-share data in New York City. The goal was to provide insights into bike usage patterns and user behavior in different locations, seasons, and weather conditions to support the Customer Growth team in building a business plan for the next year. Data used in this project:

  • NYC Citi Bike Trips
  • Census Bureau US Boundaries
  • NOAA Global Summary of the Day
  • Internal dataset containing all NYC ZIP codes

The project was separated into three phases: project planning; data preparation; and data analysis, visualization, and presentation. In the planning phase, I gathered and compiled the requirements for the project into Stakeholder Requirements, Project Requirements, and Strategy documents. From there, in the data preparation phase, I used Google BigQuery to extract the necessary bike-share data and load it into target tables. In the data analysis, visualization, and presentation phase, I inspected the data and created visualizations in Tableau to communicate the insights to stakeholders. These visualizations were placed on a comprehensive, interactive dashboard for stakeholders to access. Finally, I created an executive summary to to provide a concise overview of the project, its findings, and recommendations.

Key skills used: Data extraction, data transformation, data loading (ETL); data analysis; data visualization; SQL; Google BigQuery; Tableau; Business Intelligence (BI)

Cyclistic Case Study (October 2025)

This project was one of the final parts of the Google Data Analytics Certificate program. In this project, I was assigned a task to analyze trip data for a fictional Chicago-based bike-share company, Cyclistic, to understand how casual riders and annual members used Cyclistic bikes differently. Casual riders were those who purchased single-ride or full-day passes, and members were those who purchased an annual membership. The data used in this project were the trips from the first quarters of 2019 and 2020 from DIVVY's public data bucket. The data was cleaned and manipulated in Google Sheets and R, with visualizations created with R's ggplot package from the tidyverse.

Key skills used: Data cleaning, data analysis, data visualization, Google Sheets, R Programming

Project report: Cyclistic Case Study Report (HTML)

Analysis presentation: Cyclistic Bike Use (PPTX)
